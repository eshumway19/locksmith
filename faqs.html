Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about our services, locks, access control systems, and more.

Do access control systems require ongoing subscriptions? +
Some cloud-based access control systems do have monthly subscription fees for remote management, mobile app features, and cloud storage of access logs. However, many standalone systems operate without any subscription. We'll discuss both options and help you choose the right system based on your budget and needs during your free consultation.
Can you service and repair existing access control systems? +
Yes! We service most major brands of access control hardware. We can troubleshoot, repair, reprogram, and upgrade existing systems. Contact us with the make and model of your system and we'll let you know if we can help.
What is rekeying, and when should I have it done? +
Rekeying means changing the internal pins of your lock so that old keys no longer work and new keys are cut. You should consider rekeying when you move into a new home, after a break-in or attempted break-in, when you lose a key, after a tenant moves out, or when an employee with key access leaves your company. Rekeying is typically less expensive than replacing the entire lock.
Can all my locks be rekeyed to the same key? +
In most cases, yes. As long as your locks are the same brand or compatible brands, we can rekey them to work with a single key. This is called "keying alike" and is a very popular option for homeowners who want the convenience of one key for all their locks.
Will picking a lock damage it? +
When done by a trained professional, lock picking typically causes no damage to the lock. We use proper tools and techniques that are designed to open locks without destructive force. However, in some situations — particularly with high-security or damaged locks — other methods may be needed, which we'll discuss with you beforehand.
What's the difference between an electronic deadbolt and a smart lock? +
An electronic deadbolt uses a keypad or card reader to operate without a traditional key, but it typically doesn't connect to the internet. A smart lock adds Wi-Fi or Bluetooth connectivity, allowing you to lock/unlock remotely via smartphone app, set access schedules, receive alerts, and integrate with smart home systems like Apple HomeKit, Google Home, or Amazon Alexa. Smart locks offer more features but may require a hub or subscription.
Where should I install security cameras on my home? +
We generally recommend installing cameras on the exterior of your home rather than interior spaces. Key locations include: front door and main entry points, garage area, backyard gates, and driveways. Exterior cameras deter intruders, capture footage of anyone approaching your property, and avoid privacy concerns associated with interior monitoring. We can assess your property and recommend the optimal camera placement during a free consultation.
Can you install access control on just one door? +
Absolutely. Single-door access control systems are very common, especially for small businesses, home offices, or server rooms. We can install a standalone keypad, card reader, or even a mobile-credential reader on just one entry point. These systems can always be expanded later if your needs grow.
Can you install a security system I purchased myself? +
Yes, we can install customer-supplied equipment in most cases. Please note that because we don't supply the hardware, we are unable to offer our standard parts warranty on customer-provided equipment. However, our workmanship warranty still applies to the installation itself. Contact us with the product details before purchasing to make sure it's compatible with your setup.
Should I repair or replace a door closer? +
It depends on the age and condition of the closer. Many door closer issues — slow closing, improper latching, slamming — can be resolved with an adjustment or minor repair. If the unit is leaking hydraulic fluid, physically damaged, or consistently failing after adjustments, replacement is usually the more cost-effective solution. We'll give you an honest assessment when we look at it.
Can panic bars be repaired, or do they need to be replaced? +
Many panic bar issues can be repaired — including misalignment, worn push pads, and latching problems. Major brands have replacement parts available. However, older panic bars or those with significant structural damage may be more economical to replace entirely. Since panic hardware is a life-safety device, we always recommend professional inspection and prompt repair or replacement when issues are found.
What is a master key system? +
A master key system allows different keys to open different combinations of locks within a building or property. For example, a tenant's key opens only their unit, a manager's key opens multiple units, and a master key opens everything. These systems are ideal for apartment complexes, office buildings, schools, and hotels. We design and install custom master key systems based on your specific access needs.
Can a new access control system integrate with my existing infrastructure? +
In many cases, yes. Modern access control systems are designed to integrate with existing door hardware, electric strikes, magnetic locks, and building management systems. We can also integrate with existing camera systems, intercoms, and alarm panels in many cases. During our site assessment, we'll evaluate your current infrastructure and recommend the best integration approach.
